1
A new thing popular artists are doing is the Verified Fan pre-sales. Make sure you’re following your artist on Twitter and have signed up for their e-mail newsletters so you don’t miss the pre-sale registration.
2
Ticketmaster, LiveNation, and AXS are the leading ticket retailers. Find out which vendor your artist is using and make an account. Save your credit card information to your account.
3
Tickets go on sale at 10:00am sharp. If you have a pre-sale code, already have it copied onto the clipboard. Make sure you have enough money on your card for everyone in your party so you can get tickets together.
4
Paperless ticketing is easier, more efficient, and better for the environment. Have your tickets e-mailed to you or download your retailer's smartphone app. The venue workers will scan your ticket straight from your phone.
 
5
If you are traveling more than 3-4 hours, it may be useful to rent a vehicle. The cheapest option you can rent from Enterprise will be about $100 (with a deposit fee that will be refunded to you when the car is returned.)
6
Always bring an extra $20 in cash for parking. If available, go ahead and pre-pay for parking when purchasing your tickets.
 
7
The hardcore community will say not to wear a bands' merch to their show because it's not very hardcore. But I say: what do you think the band is going to remember more — your outfit or how happy it made them to see how many fans in the audience are wearing their merch?
8
Wear something with pockets to hold your phone, keys, etc. If you must bring a bag, bring a cross-body or one with backpack straps so you won't lose it.
 
9
So you got pit tickets, huh? Here's my best GA tips:
  • Know the fandom. How likely are the fans to camp out to get barrier? How long are they willing to camp out?
  • Don't expect to get close to the stage if you aren't willing to do some waiting in line.
  • Call the venue and see what time they'll allow a line to form. Show up before the time they tell you.
  • I'm serious. There will be fans there that have been in line since Wednesday. You won't beat them.
  • Some venues don't allow camping. There will be an unofficial line around the block. Fans will come up with some sort of number system. Whether you want to arrive early to join that line or arrive at the time the venue will allow an "official" line to form is up to you.
  • Find out where the nearest parking area is before you arrive. Will you have quick access to your car to retrieve and deposit supplies as needed? If not, only bring what you intend to carry into the venue with you.
  • If you will be close to your car, milk it. Bring a tent*. Bring sleeping bags. Bring fold-out chairs. Bring a change of clothes.
  • Bring high-protein snacks such as nuts or jerky.
  • Hydrate, hydrate, hydrate.
  • Wear sunscreen!
  • Bring a portable phone charger. Bring a portable charge for the portable phone charger.
  • Bring ibuprofen. You'll need it.
  • Make friends in line. They'll save your spot when you run pee.
  • Lastly, if you have a panic disorder or are extremely claustrophobic, please consider the risks before you decide to stand in the pit. The space will be very crammed and hot, and there will be lots of shoving. Your well-being is far more important, and I can promise you the band would agree with me.
 
* Not all venues allow tents.
 
10
Be respectful! The artists are people, too. If you wouldn't want to be grabbed inappropriately by a stranger, they probably wouldn't either.
11
Be respectful part two: Everyone in the audience is there for the same reason. Please be kind to the other fans.
"F%!#ing cool it!" – AJ
12
Try to keep your phone down. Limit yourself to 5-10 photos and only video your absolute favorite parts of your favorite songs. No one on your Snapchat is really watching these, anyway. The artists wanna see you, not your phone!
